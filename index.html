What is a Group Discussion (GD)?

A group discussion is a platform where individuals express their ideas and opinions on a given topic within a group setting.

Do's in a GD:

  • Start the discussion confidently if you can.
  • Listen actively and speak logically.
  • Respect others' points of view.
  • Support your statements with examples or facts.
  • Maintain eye contact with group members, not just the moderators.

Don'ts in a GD:

  • Don’t interrupt others while they are speaking.
  • Don’t be aggressive or shout.
  • Don’t only agree or disagree without giving reasons.
  • Don’t dominate the discussion.
  • Don’t look at the moderators constantly.

Different Types of Group Discussions:

  • Topic-based GD: General, factual, or abstract topics (e.g., "Impact of Technology on Youth").
  • Case Study GD: A situation is given; you need to solve or analyze it.
  • Group Task GD: Task-based discussions requiring group planning or strategy (e.g., survival situations).
  • Opinion-based GD: Topics asking for viewpoints on social, political, or economic issues.

Idea Generation Techniques for GDs:

  • PESTLE Analysis: Analyze Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, and Environmental factors related to the topic.
  • SWOT Analysis: Think about Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats.
  • Mind Mapping: Quickly draw links between ideas or keywords to expand thoughts.
  • 5W1H Technique: Frame questions using What, Why, When, Where, Who, and How to explore the topic deeply.

Quick GD Preparation Plan:

  • 1. Understand the Topic: Take 30 seconds to understand the topic clearly. Think about its basic definition, examples, and real-life connections.
  • 2. Prepare Key Points: Quickly structure 2–3 strong points in your mind. Use techniques like SWOT, PESTLE, or 5W1H to expand your thinking if needed.
  • 3. Perform with Confidence: Speak clearly, listen actively, respect others' views, and contribute meaningfully. Try to initiate or summarize if possible.

"Understand ➔ Prepare ➔ Perform — that's the smart GD strategy!"
